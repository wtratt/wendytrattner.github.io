ABOUT ME

I grew up in Milwaukee, Wisconsin, and lived with various guardians for most of my childhood. In middle school, I spent my time reading books and thinking about what I needed to do in order to find happiness. I found myself drawn to many topics in science, including cellular biology, black holes, and climate change.

In high school, I became a competitive Lincoln-Douglas debater, and was introduced to a large variety of issues in philosophy, law, policy, medicine, technology, and the environment. Toward the end of high school, I developed a desire for hands-on learning and wanted to apply my knowledge meaningfully, so I found a way to intern at MIT's Koch Institute for Integrative Cancer Research.

My love of science eventually brought me to MIT as an undergraduate, where I've been exploring my many interests. I learned that even with no prior experience, I could learn to build useful things, and I declared mechanical engineering as my major.
