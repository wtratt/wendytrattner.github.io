MY STORY

I grew up in Milwaukee, Wisconsin. When I turned 7, my mother died, and I was sent to live with various guardians throughout the rest of my childhood. I learned to take care of myself, and spent my time reading books and thinking about what I needed to do in order to find happiness. During middle school, I found myself drawn to many topics in science, including cellular biology, black holes, and climate change.

In high school, I became a varsity Lincoln-Douglas debater, swimmer, and rower. I worked at a local retail store, and found a way to intern for a summer at MIT's cancer research institute.

My love of science eventually brought me to MIT as an undergraduate, where I've been exploring my many interests. I learned that even with no prior experience, I could be an engineer and build useful things, so I declared mechanical engineering as my major.
