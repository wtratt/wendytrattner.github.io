MY STORY

I have an immense drive to make an impact and a desire for constant self improvement. But how did I become this way?

I grew up in Milwaukee, Wisconsin. When I turned 7, my mother died, and I spent the next chapter of my life living with various aunts and uncles, some of whom did not treat me kindly. As a result, I largely took care of myself. In middle school, I spent my time reading books, learning about interesting topics in science, and thinking about what I needed to do in order to find happiness.

In high school, I learned to take advantage of opportunities and become a self-sufficient adult. I became a varsity Lincoln-Douglas debater and rower, and interned at MIT's cancer research institute in the summer. I took control of my finances and legal matters.

My love of science eventually brought me to MIT as an undergraduate, where I've been exploring my many interests. I learned that I could be an engineer and build useful things, even with no prior experience. I declared mechanical engineering as my major so that I could continue to build cool and useful things.

Throughout my entire life, I've had to be completely self-motivated. Through my own experience of what can be accomplished through pure determination, I've developed high standards and ambitions for myself. I believe that I can create meaning for myself by contributing to the betterment of society, and I hope to continue to learn and grow on this mission.
